# NoAPI Studio: The Sharpest Problem to Put on the Naitive / H-Farm Application

## TL;DR
- **The single best problem + named group:** Put **"accounts-payable and procurement operations specialists at European mid-market manufacturers and distributors (≈€50M–€500M revenue, 50–500 staff) who log into 15–50 buyer/supplier and carrier portals that have no API, to download invoices, remittance advice, PO statuses and delivery confirmations by hand"** in Section 1. This group is precisely nameable, the pain is daily and quantified (per Ardent Partners, the average AP department spends nearly one quarter of its time handling supplier inquiries; one finance team reported saving 20+ hours/week after portal work was removed), budgets already exist (RPA + AP-automation line items), and it sits squarely in EU mid-market where Naitive/H-Farm has a right to play.
- **The strongest "what changed":** The convergence in the last ~18 months of (1) **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**, launched by Anthropic in November 2024 and now an industry standard, giving a *standard typed interface* for agent tools; (2) **traffic-based black-box API discovery** (APISENSOR, 95.92% group-accuracy precision) that reconstructs a portal's hidden internal API from observed authorized human traffic; and (3) **cheap, reliable LLM tool-calling + computer-use models** as a *fallback*, not the primary path. This is exactly what makes "record once → compile to a typed, monitored action" newly buildable.
- **The honest verdict on the thesis:** The core "no-API typed actions" wedge is correct **but the defensible product is the reliability discipline around it** — continuous validation, automatic repair, versioning, and risk-gated execution — *not* the one-shot "turn a website into an API" generation that ≥6 funded startups (Deck, Composio, Arcade, Skyvern, Browser Use, Reworkd) already do. Lead with the **repair-and-validation flywheel**, because every benchmark and practitioner signal shows that agents and RPA bots *break*, and nobody owns the "keep it working" layer.

## Key Findings

**1. Agents are nowhere near production-reliable, and the gap is structural, not just model quality.**
- OpenAI's Computer-Using Agent (Operator), launched January 2025, achieved **38.1% on OSWorld** (full computer use), **58.1% on WebArena** (vs. prior state-of-the-art 36.2%), and **87% on WebVoyager** — and OpenAI led publicly with the browser numbers, not the OSWorld one. Per OpenAI's own write-up, **human performance on OSWorld is 72.4%.** By September 2025 Claude Sonnet 4.5 reached 61.4% on OSWorld.
- On WebArena, the best single-agent completion rate was 61.7% (IBM CUGA, Feb 2025) vs. ~78% human. On the harder WebChoreArena, top models drop to ~37.8%.
- The compounding-error math is the killer: an agent with 85% per-step accuracy completes a 10-step workflow only ~20% of the time. The APEX-Agents 2026 benchmark reportedly found even the best models completed only ~24% of real-world tasks on the first attempt (secondary reporting — verify before headline use).
- WAREX (arXiv 2510.03285) and Microsoft's WABER show success rates plummet by 30%+ once realistic web instability (network errors, 5xx, JS delays, popups) is injected — i.e., lab scores massively overstate real-world reliability.
- The Princeton "Towards a Science of AI Agent Reliability" paper (covered by Fortune, March 2026) found even the best models (Claude Opus 4.5, Gemini 3 Pro) scored only ~85% on a composite reliability metric, with Gemini 3 Pro at just 25% on "avoiding catastrophic mistakes."
- **Gartner (press release, June 25, 2025, Anushree Verma):** "Over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by the end of 2027, due to escalating costs, unclear business value or inadequate risk controls" — based on a January 2025 poll of 3,412 webinar attendees. Gartner also estimates only ~130 of the thousands of agentic-AI vendors are "real."

**2. RPA — the incumbent solution — is economically broken precisely because of UI fragility.**
- Maintenance is the dominant cost. Industry analyses (Everest Group, Deloitte) repeatedly put RPA maintenance at 30–40% of implementation savings/budget, with some vendor estimates as high as 70–75%; 30–50% of RPA projects are abandoned within two years. Forrester research is widely cited that ~52% of organizations struggle to *scale* their RPA programs (note: this is a scaling-difficulty statistic, not a "52% of projected ROI" figure — earlier framings of this claim should not be used).
- The cause is exactly the thesis's wedge: "When a software vendor updates their UI… every bot that touches that interface breaks. A single Salesforce update can break dozens of RPA bots." Deloitte's 2025 survey: the average enterprise RPA bot hits exceptions on 15–25% of executions.
- Practitioner ground truth is overwhelming: the UiPath community forum is full of threads like "Broken selectors after Chrome/Edge update," "Selectors breaking after every application reload," and "Unable to get selectors after a Windows update." This is a chronic, recurring, named pain — not a hypothetical.

**3. The "what changed" capabilities are real, recent, and citable.**
- **MCP:** launched Nov 2024 by Anthropic; adopted by OpenAI (Mar 2025), Google DeepMind (Apr 2025), Microsoft (Build 2025); donated to the Linux Foundation's newly-formed Agentic AI Foundation (co-founded with Block and OpenAI) in Dec 2025. Server downloads grew from ~100k (Nov 2024) to 8M+ (Apr 2025); Anthropic reported 97M monthly SDK downloads and 10,000+ active public MCP servers by the one-year anniversary. This standardizes the *interface* a reliability layer would expose.
- **Typed actions / web verbs:** the position paper "Web Verbs: Typed Abstractions for Reliable Task Composition on the Agentic Web" (arXiv 2602.17245, Feb 2026) argues exactly the NoAPI Studio thesis — shift from click-based browsing to typed functions with structured inputs/outputs, preconditions, postconditions, policy tags, and logging hooks, "whether implemented through APIs or robust client-side workflows." This is direct academic validation of the wedge.
- **Traffic-based API discovery:** APISENSOR (arXiv 2603.23852) reconstructs a portal's APIs from runtime traffic unsupervised at **95.92% group-accuracy precision** — the technical basis for "observed internal network route" as a preferred execution path over brittle DOM clicking.

**4. The competitive field is crowded on generation but empty on the reliability discipline.**
- **Direct "turn portal into API" players:** **Deck** (Montreal; **$16.5M total, $12M Series A led by Infinity Ventures, April 16, 2025** per TechCrunch; site: "With one secure API, we turn the entire web into a user-permissioned read and write interface," CEO Yves-Gabriel Leboeuf: "Ninety-five percent of platforms still don't offer reliable APIs" — the **strongest direct competitor**, but consumer/fintech-data framed); **Skyvern** ($2.7M seed; vision-based browser automation with a Planner/Actor/Validator architecture); **Browser Use** ($17M seed, March 2025, YC W25, led by Felicis); **Browserbase/Stagehand** ($40M Series B at $300M post-money, June 2025; "self-healing" action caching); **Reworkd** ("the unified API for the internet," ~$4M total).
- **MCP tool/integration layer:** **Composio** ($25M Series A, July 2025; 1,000+ toolkits, "maintains and updates all integrations automatically"); **Arcade.dev** ($72M total incl. $60M Series A June 2026; auth-first MCP runtime with a tool-eval framework); **Manufact** (YC S2025, $6.3M; MCP deployment/observability cloud); **Inconvo** (YC; "build MCP servers for SaaS customers").
- **Incumbents going agentic:** **UiPath** (Maestro orchestration, a "Healing agent" that adapts to UI changes, MCP support, CTO targeting "95%+ agent accuracy"; but market cap ~$5.3B in mid-2026, down ~85% from its ~$35B 2021 IPO-era peak); **Zapier, Workato, Make, and n8n** ($180M Series C at a $2.5B valuation, October 2025).
- **The gap nobody owns:** continuous canary testing + schema/postcondition validation + automatic repair when the DOM changes + API-style versioning + risk-gated execution (read autonomous, write logged, destructive human-gated), delivered as an *ongoing discipline* rather than a one-shot compile. Browserbase markets "self-healing" and UiPath a "Healing agent," but neither sells the full reliability-SLA layer with a proprietary cross-customer repair-data flywheel. This is the defensible position.

**5. The EU mid-market angle is a genuine wedge, not a fig leaf.**
- The EU AI Act's high-risk obligations (Article 12 logging, Article 13 transparency, Article 14 human oversight, Article 15 robustness) and GDPR Article 22 (solely-automated decisions) reward a product whose entire architecture is *checkable execution traces, postconditions, audit logs and human-in-the-loop gates*. A reliability/governance layer is compliance-native by design.
- European mid-market manufacturers (the Bosch/Siemens/Schneider tier and their SME suppliers) face the EU Data Act's data-access mandates and operate across fragmented national portals — a structurally portal-heavy, API-poor environment with real budgets and a sovereignty preference for an EU-built tool.

## Details

### Section 1 — Problem Discovery (the ~350-word answer to put in the form)

**(a) The problem and the specific group.** Accounts-payable and procurement operations specialists at European mid-market manufacturers and distributors (≈€50M–€500M revenue) spend a large share of every week logging into 15–50 distinct buyer/supplier/carrier portals — each with its own login, layout and naming — to manually download invoices, remittance advice, PO and delivery statuses, and to re-key that data into their ERP. These portals deliberately have no API. (Billtrust's Amanda Wilson: "I have numerous customers who are trapped in a cycle of managing 20, 30 or even 50 different AP portal requirements," noting "over 200 distinct AP portals.") Adjacent equally-nameable groups: ocean-freight operations coordinators at mid-market shippers/forwarders copying ETAs from MSC/Maersk/CMA CGM/Hapag-Lloyd portals into spreadsheets ("two hours a day"), and prior-authorization/referral coordinators at specialty medical groups doing payer-portal data entry.

**(b) What changed.** Three things became possible in ~24 months: MCP (Nov 2024) standardized the typed interface agents call; traffic-based black-box API discovery (APISENSOR, 95.92% precision, 2026) can reconstruct a portal's hidden internal API from observed authorized human traffic — so you don't have to click the UI; and cheap reliable LLM tool-calling plus computer-use models give a graceful *fallback* path. The named capability to cite: **MCP-standardized typed actions backed by traffic-discovered private APIs.**

**(c) Why it matters.** Maintenance eats 30–40% of RPA budgets and 30–50% of RPA projects are abandoned in two years, almost entirely because UIs change and bots break. Agents fail 4–8 times out of 10 on real tasks. The cost is paid in headcount, late-payment penalties, and stalled automation programs.

**(d) Direction — and why not the obvious place.** The obvious answer is "make a better browser agent / generate the integration once." That is where the funding and the failure both concentrate: one-shot automation that breaks on the next UI change. The answer lies one layer up — in **the reliability discipline** (validate continuously, repair automatically, version like an API, gate by risk). The moat is the proprietary repair-data flywheel: every break you fix across customers makes the next repair faster.

### Section 2 — Discovery Log (~350-word answer)

**What to investigate / what we did.** We triangulated four evidence streams. (1) *Academic:* WebArena/OSWorld/WebVoyager leaderboards and the reliability-specific WAREX (arXiv 2510.03285) and WABER papers, which show 30%+ success-rate collapse under realistic web instability; the "Web Verbs" position paper (arXiv 2602.17245) which independently argues the typed-actions thesis; APISENSOR (arXiv 2603.23852) for traffic-based API discovery; and OS-Harm (arXiv 2506.14866) for agent security. (2) *Industry/market:* Forrester/Deloitte/Gartner RPA economics; MCP adoption metrics; AI-agents market sizing; competitor funding. (3) *Practitioner forums:* the UiPath community forum's recurring "broken selectors after browser/Windows update" threads, Hacker News and Reddit complaints that agents "get office tasks wrong around 70% of the time," and vendor-portal-fatigue quotes. (4) *Vertical pain:* AP supplier-portal time studies, ocean-freight manual-tracking accounts, and prior-authorization burden data (CMS: completing prior authorizations costs providers $20–50/hour and takes an average of 13 hours per week — nearly $34,000 and 700 hours a year per provider; the 13 hrs/week and 39 PAs/physician/week figures come from the 2024 AMA Prior Authorization Physician Survey).

**What would surprise a founder.** (i) The incumbents already concede the problem — UiPath ships a "Healing agent" and Gartner predicts 60% of RPA vendors will add "computer use" by 2027 — yet none sell *reliability-as-a-discipline*. (ii) The cheapest, most reliable execution path is usually NOT the browser at all but the portal's own undocumented internal API, which is now machine-discoverable. (iii) "Self-healing" is marketed everywhere but proven nowhere; WAREX shows it doesn't hold under real instability. (iv) The most acute willingness-to-pay may be in healthcare PA and freight, not the AP wedge we'd lead with.

**What AI specifically did.** AI ran ~18 web searches plus a focused funding/market sub-investigation; surfaced and parsed five primary arXiv papers; extracted verbatim practitioner complaints from the UiPath forum and HN; reconciled conflicting market figures across research firms (RPA 2025 estimates ranged $4.68B–$28.31B); and discovered three previously-unknown direct competitors (Deck, Manufact, Inconvo) that a manual scan would likely have missed.

### Section 3 — Trade-off Report (~300-word answer)

**Alternative problems considered and rejected.** (1) *A pure browser-agent reliability/observability tool for agent developers* — rejected as the lead because it sells to a thin, early developer market and competes head-on with Browserbase, LangSmith and Datadog's AI observability; better as a later expansion. (2) *Agent security (prompt injection / OS-Harm)* — real and worsening (OS-Harm shows all frontier models comply with many misuse queries and are vulnerable to static prompt injection), but it's a feature regulators will force into every platform, not a standalone wedge with a nameable paying buyer today. (3) *Generic "turn any website into an API"* — rejected because ≥6 funded startups already do one-shot generation; the durable value is keeping it working. (4) *MCP tool maintenance for SaaS-with-APIs* — Composio/Arcade own this; the un-served space is apps *without* APIs.

**Hidden assumptions not yet proven.** (i) That traffic-discovered private APIs are stable enough and legally usable (ToS/anti-bot risk). (ii) That customers will grant authorized credential access and accept liability for automated actions on their accounts. (iii) That a repair-data flywheel actually compounds across heterogeneous portals rather than being bespoke per site. (iv) That mid-market buyers will pay for reliability SLAs rather than tolerate cheap, brittle automation.

**When this would be the WRONG problem.** If portals rapidly adopt official APIs (Gartner: 80%+ of enterprise apps API-accessible by 2028; CMS mandates payer FHIR prior-auth APIs by 2027), the no-API surface shrinks. If frontier computer-use models cross a reliability threshold (some OSWorld submissions already exceed the 72.4% human baseline) the "repair" need could erode. And if incumbents (UiPath Maestro, MCP gateways) bundle continuous validation natively, the standalone wedge closes. The bet is that UI churn and the long tail of legacy portals outlast all three — which the 30-year persistence of screen-scraping suggests.

**Stage 1 — Put this in the form (now).**
- **Problem + group (Section 1a):** "AP/procurement ops specialists at European mid-market manufacturers/distributors using 15–50 supplier and carrier portals without APIs." It is specific, nameable, EU-anchored, and budget-backed.
- **What changed (Section 1b):** "MCP-standardized typed actions (Nov 2024) + traffic-based private-API discovery (APISENSOR, 95.92% precision) make it possible to record an authorized human workflow once and compile it into a monitored, typed tool — choosing the portal's own internal API over fragile clicking."

**Stage 2 — Validation benchmarks before committing (next 4–8 weeks).**
- Land 3–5 design-partner finance teams; instrument the real numbers (portals/team, hours/week, % of automations that break per month, mean-time-to-repair). **Threshold to proceed:** ≥10 hrs/week/team recovered AND ≥1 break/automation/month (proves the repair flywheel has fuel).
- Prove the "private API > browser" hypothesis on 5 real portals: can you discover and stabilize an internal route, and does it survive a UI change that breaks a Playwright script? **Threshold:** ≥60% of target portals expose a usable discovered route.

**Ranked backup groups for Section 1a:** (1) Prior-auth/referral coordinators at US specialty medical groups; (2) ocean-freight operations coordinators at mid-market shippers/forwarders; (3) compliance analysts completing security questionnaires across vendor portals; (4) e-commerce marketplace ops managers across seller-central portals.

## Caveats
- **Market-size figures vary wildly by source** (2025 RPA estimates spanned $4.68B–$28.31B; iPaaS $9B–$17.6B). Use Gartner's iPaaS (~$9B in 2024 → $17B by 2028) and MarketsandMarkets' AI-agents ($5.25B 2024 → $52.62B 2030) as the most defensible; treat single-source RPA-maintenance "70–75%" claims as upper-bound vendor estimates, with 30–40% as the conservative anchor.
- **The widely-circulated "Forrester: 52% of projected ROI" claim could not be verified;** the closest sourced Forrester figure is that ~52% of organizations struggle to *scale* their RPA programs. Do not quote the ROI framing without reverifying the original Forrester report.
- **Several vendor blogs (Coasty, lowtouch, dextralabs) are talking their own book** ("RPA is dead"); their underlying Gartner/Forrester/Deloitte citations are usable but should be verified against primary reports before quoting in a pitch.
- **Legal/ToS risk is the biggest unproven assumption:** automating logins and discovering internal APIs on third-party portals may violate terms of service or anti-bot measures; the "authorized human workflow" framing mitigates but does not eliminate this. Validate with design partners and counsel before scaling.
- **The "self-healing/repair" claim is the make-or-break technical bet.** It is marketed by incumbents but unproven at scale (WAREX); if NoAPI Studio cannot demonstrably repair faster/cheaper than a human re-recording the workflow, the moat collapses.
- Some cited reliability statistics (APEX-Agents 24%, Carnegie Mellon ~70% failure) come from secondary reporting; verify the primary source before headline use.
